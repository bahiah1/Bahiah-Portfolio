My Backstory

Hi, I'm Nur Bahiah Binti Zaykarnain, but most people call me Bahi.

This coming September 2025, I’ll be turning 20, which honestly feels a bit surreal. Deep down, I still feel like that 15-year-old trying to figure life out. But somehow, here I am, in my second year of diploma studies at Universiti Teknologi MARA (UiTM) Kedah, continuing to grow in ways I never expected.

I was born and raised in Tanah Merah, Kelantan, a place filled with warmth, memories, and, of course, delicious food (nothing beats nasi kerabu from home!). Moving to Kedah for university was a big change. It meant leaving the familiar behind my family, friends, and comfort zone, but it’s been one of the most important steps in shaping who I am today. I’ve learned to be more independent, adaptable, and open-minded, especially when facing new environments and people.

When I’m not caught up with classes or assignments, you’ll usually find me playing games, listening to music, or binge-watching K-dramas or K-movies (yes, I cry every single time, even when I already know what’s coming!). I also treasure quiet moments, especially late at night when the world is still, or during sunset. Those moments help me slow down, reflect, and recharge.

As a person, I’d describe myself as calm, introverted, and a little shy at first, but once I get comfortable, I love making people laugh and having deep, meaningful conversations. I value honesty, kindness, and personal growth, and I try to live by the motto:It’s okay to grow slowly, as long as you’re growin

Adjusting to university life wasn’t easy at first. Being away from my family and managing everything on my own was a huge change. However, over time, I developed a routine that provided me with structure and helped me stay focused. One of the biggest things that helped was creating a weekly schedule that balanced my academic and personal life.

Beyond academics, I have dreams of traveling, especially to South Korea, not just because of my love for K-culture, but to truly experience the beauty of another country, try the food, and maybe even pick up the language. I also hope to keep growing into someone brave enough to chase her dreams, no matter how big or small.

So, that’s me, Bahi, a small-town girl with big hopes, learning, evolving, and taking it one step at a time.
